[size=4][b]What is UCM?[/b][/size]

Ultimate Camera Mod is a standalone Windows app that gives you full control over Crimson Desert's third-person camera. Pick from hand-tuned presets, build your own with sliders, or go deep and tweak every single camera parameter in the game.

[size=3][color=#d98f40]Why UCM?[/color][/size]

[list]
[*][b]Game-patch proof[/b] - UCM builds your camera from your own game files. When Crimson Desert updates, just hit Install and you're back in action. No waiting on mod authors to release updated files.[/*]
[*][b]Three levels of control[/b] - Quick sliders for casual users, Fine Tune cards for enthusiasts, God Mode raw XML for the deep end. Use whichever level fits you.[/*]
[*][b]19 languages[/b] - Full UI localization with live switching. English, Korean, Japanese, Chinese (Simplified/Traditional), Thai, Indonesian, Turkish, Polish, Italian, Swedish, Norwegian, Danish, Finnish, German, French, Spanish, Portuguese Brazilian, Russian.[/*]
[*][b]Community preset catalog[/b] - Browse and download camera presets from other players directly in-app. One click to install someone else's setup.[/*]
[*][b]Mod manager compatible[/b] - Export JSON patches for [url=https://www.nexusmods.com/crimsondesert/mods/113]JSON Mod Manager[/url] or [url=https://www.nexusmods.com/crimsondesert/mods/207]CDUMM[/url]. UCM fits into your existing mod workflow.[/*]
[*][b]Shareable presets[/b] - Export your camera as a .ucmpreset file and share it with anyone. They import it, done.[/*]
[*][b]Safe and reversible[/b] - No DLL injection, no memory hacking, no internet required. Pure game-file modification with automatic backup. One click to restore vanilla at any time.[/*]
[*][b]Single exe, zero dependencies[/b] - No installer. No runtime to download. Place it anywhere and run.[/*]
[*][b]Open source under MIT[/b] - Every line of code is on GitHub. Build from source if you want.[/*]
[/list]

[center][size=5][b][color=#ffd966]Three-Tier Editor[/color][/b][/size]
[size=3]Start simple. Go deeper when you want to.[/size][/center]

UCM v3 replaces the old single-tab layout with three progressive tiers of control. Use whichever level fits your needs.

[size=3][color=#d98f40]Tier 1: UCM Quick[/color][/size]

The fast lane. Sliders for distance, height, and horizontal shift. Dropdown for FoV. Checkboxes for features. A live camera preview updates in real time as you adjust. This is where most people will live.

[*][b]Height[/b] (-1.6 to 1.5) adjusts vertical position[/*]
[*][b]Horizontal Shift[/b] (-3.0 to 3.0) moves the character's screen position using proportional scaling across all zoom levels[/*]

[center][size=5][b][color=#ffd966]Multi-Format Export & Mod Manager Support[/color][/b][/size]

7 FoV options from vanilla 40 up to 80, with recommendations for different aspect ratios:

[list]
[*]No change (40) - Vanilla[/*]
[*]+10 (50) - Minimal increase, good for 16:9[/*]
[*]+15 (55) - Subtle improvement[/*]

[*]+40 (80) - Extreme wide, slight fisheye[/*]

FoV is applied universally across all camera states (guard, aim, mount, glide, cinematic) so there are no jarring transitions when the game switches states.

[size=3][color=#d98f40]Steadycam Smoothing[/color][/size]

Completely revamped camera smoothing system covering 30+ camera states. Drastically reduces the jolting and jarring transitions between movement states that plague the vanilla camera. The community can fine-tune individual states through God Mode.

[size=3][color=#d98f40]Lock-on System[/color][/size]

Two new controls for lock-on combat:
[list]
[*][b]Lock-on Zoom[/b] - A slider from -60% to +60% that lets you zoom in tight on your target or pull back for battlefield awareness. Independent of Steadycam.[/*]
[*][b]Lock-on Auto-Rotate[/b] - Toggle off the camera snap that whips around to face your target. Keep camera control in your hands during combat.[/*]
[/list]

[size=3][color=#d98f40]Horizontal Shift Everywhere[/color][/size]

Your horizontal shift setting is respected across the entire game:
[list]
[*]All on-foot states[/*]
[*]All mounts: horse, elephant, wyvern, canoe, warmachine, broom[/*]
[*]All aim/skill abilities: lantern, bow, blinding flash, and more[/*]
[*]Proportional scaling keeps the character at the same screen position across all zoom levels[/*]
[/list]

[size=3][color=#d98f40]Sacred God Mode (v3.1)[/color][/size]

The biggest addition since v3 launched. When you edit a value in God Mode, it becomes [color=#00ff00]sacred[/color] - permanently protected from UCM's automatic rebuilds when you change Quick or Fine Tune settings. Your hand-tuned values are never overwritten. Locked sliders show tooltips explaining which sacred value is protecting them.

This means you can use UCM Quick for the broad strokes, then drop into God Mode to perfect specific values, and those values stay exactly where you put them.

[size=4][b]How It Works[/b][/size]

[list=1]
[*]UCM locates the game's PAZ archive containing [font=Courier]playercamerapreset.xml[/font][/*]
[*]Creates a backup of the original file (only once, never overwrites a clean backup)[/*]
[*]Decrypts the archive entry using ChaCha20 + Jenkins hash key derivation[/*]
[*]Decompresses via LZ4[/*]
[*]Applies your camera values as pure value-edits (no structural XML injection)[/*]
[*]Re-compresses, re-encrypts, and writes the modified entry back into the archive[/*]
[/list]

No external dependencies at runtime. Single self-contained executable. No memory injection, no DLLs, no hooks.

[size=4][b]FAQ[/b][/size]

[b]Q: Will this get me banned?[/b]
A: UCM modifies offline data files only. It does not touch game memory, inject code, or interact with running processes. No mod author can guarantee compatibility with anti-cheat systems, so use your own discretion in any online/multiplayer modes.

[b]Q: Can I use this with other mods?[/b]
A: Yes, as long as they don't modify the same PAZ archive entry ([font=Courier]playercamerapreset.xml[/font]). UCM will detect if your game files have already been modified. The JSON export format is designed to work alongside other mods through mod managers.

[b]Q: The game updated and my camera reset.[/b]
A: Normal. Game updates overwrite modded files. Re-open UCM and click Install. Your settings are saved automatically.

[b]Q: My antivirus flagged the exe.[/b]
A: Known false positive with self-contained .NET applications. The exe is built from fully open-source code under MIT license. You can review every line on GitHub, verify with VirusTotal, or build from source yourself.

[b]Q: What's the difference between Managed and Full Manual Control presets?[/b]
A: Managed presets let UCM handle camera rules (FoV consistency, mount sync, steadycam, etc.) based on your Quick/Fine Tune settings. Full Manual Control presets give you a raw XML canvas in God Mode with no UCM rules applied.

[b]Q: How do I share my camera setup?[/b]
A: Export as a .ucmpreset file and share it directly. Or export as JSON for mod managers. Other UCM users can import your .ucmpreset through the Import dialog.

[b]Q: What does horizontal shift 0 mean?[/b]
A: 0 is the vanilla camera position (character slightly left of center). 0.5 centers the character on screen. The shift uses proportional scaling so the character holds position across all zoom levels.

[size=4][b]Community & Sharing[/b][/size]

[size=3][color=#ffc107]The camera isn't solved. That's the point.[/color][/size]

Crimson Desert has over 150 camera states, each with dozens of parameters. No single developer can tune all of that for every playstyle and display. UCM was built with sharing at its core.

That's why I created the [b].ucmpreset[/b] file format. It captures your entire camera configuration - every slider, every checkbox, every God Mode tweak - in a single file. Export yours, hand it to someone, they import it and get your exact setup. No guesswork, no manual copying.

And it's not limited to UCM users. If you find someone online who uploaded a raw [b]0.paz[/b] archive or a [b]playercamerapreset.xml[/b] file, you can import that too. UCM reads it, loads the values into the editor, and you can tweak from there. Whatever format the camera data comes in, UCM can work with it.

[size=3][color=#d98f40]The Community Catalog[/color][/size]

UCM has a built-in preset catalog that ships community presets directly to your app. No downloading from websites, no dragging files around. Open the catalog, pick a preset, and it's yours.

If you've built a camera setup you're proud of, submit it. DM me here on Nexus or open a PR on [url=https://github.com/FitzDegenhub/UltimateCameraMod]GitHub[/url] with your .ucmpreset file, and it'll show up in everyone's catalog automatically.
